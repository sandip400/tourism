Uttar Pradesh: Land of Ancient Civilizations

Uttar Pradesh, India's most populous state, is a treasure trove of history, culture, and spirituality. This comprehensive 12-day itinerary takes you on an immersive journey through the state's most significant destinations, from the magnificent Taj Mahal to the sacred ghats of Varanasi, the birthplace of Lord Ram in Ayodhya, and the spiritual confluence at Prayagraj.

Day 1

Arrival in Lucknow & City Introduction

Morning: Arrival & Check-in

Arrive at Chaudhary Charan Singh International Airport in Lucknow. Transfer to your hotel and check-in. Take some time to rest and refresh after your journey.

Accommodation: Taj Mahal Lucknow, Lebua Lucknow, or Vivanta Lucknow for luxury stays; Comfort Inn or Lineage Hotel for mid-range options.

Afternoon: Culinary Walk

Begin your Lucknow experience with a guided culinary walk through the famous Chowk area. Sample Lucknow's renowned Awadhi cuisine, including kebabs, biryani, and the famous Tunday kebabs. Learn about the rich culinary heritage of the city.

Lunch recommendation: Try the legendary Tunday Kebabi or Idris Biryani for authentic Awadhi flavors.

Evening: Bara Imambara Visit

Visit the magnificent Bara Imambara, an architectural marvel built in 1784. Explore its central hall (one of the largest vaulted chambers in the world), the famous Bhulbhulaiya (labyrinth), and the Asfi Mosque. Enjoy the panoramic view of the city from the top.

Dinner: Enjoy dinner at a heritage restaurant like Oudhyana or Royal CafÃ©, trying specialties like Galouti Kebab, Kakori Kebab, and Sheermal bread.

Day 1 Tips
  • Wear comfortable shoes as you'll be walking through the labyrinth of Bara Imambara.
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites like the Asfi Mosque.
  • The best time to visit Bara Imambara is late afternoon when the lighting is perfect for photography.
  • Consider hiring a local guide at Bara Imambara to learn about its fascinating history and architecture.
Day 2

Exploring Lucknow's Heritage

Morning: Residency Complex

Visit The Residency, a group of buildings that served as the residence for the British Resident General. Now in ruins, it's an important historical site related to the 1857 Indian Rebellion. Explore the museum, cemetery, and the well-maintained gardens.

Activity: Take a guided tour to understand the significance of this historical site in India's freedom struggle.

Afternoon: Chota Imambara & Rumi Darwaza

Visit the Chota Imambara (Hussainabad Imambara), known for its beautiful chandeliers and mirror work. Then see the magnificent Rumi Darwaza, a 60-foot tall gateway considered one of the finest examples of Awadhi architecture.

Lunch: Enjoy lunch at Dastarkhwan or Wahid Biryani, famous for their authentic Awadhi cuisine.

Evening: Hazratganj & Shopping

Explore Hazratganj, Lucknow's main shopping area. Browse through shops selling Chikan embroidery work, a specialty of Lucknow. Visit the State Emporium for authentic handicrafts and souvenirs.

Dinner: Dine at The Falaknuma or Capoor's Hotel in Hazratganj, trying local specialties like Nihari and Kulfi for dessert.

About Lucknow

Lucknow, the capital of Uttar Pradesh, is known as the "City of Nawabs" and is famous for its Awadhi cuisine, intricate embroidery, and elegant manners. The city flourished during the 18th and 19th centuries under the Nawabs of Awadh, who were great patrons of culinary and artistic developments. Lucknow's unique culture, known as "Ganga-Jamuni tehzeeb," represents a harmonious blend of Hindu and Muslim traditions.

Capital of Awadhi cuisine
Famous for Chikan embroidery
Rich Nawabi heritage
Day 3

Lucknow to Ayodhya

Morning: Lucknow's Religious Sites

Visit the Jama Masjid, one of the oldest mosques in Lucknow, known for its beautiful architecture. Then explore the Chattar Manzil, once a palace for the royal ladies of the Awadh court, now housing the Central Drug Research Institute.

Activity: Take a morning walk along the Gomti Riverfront to see the city come to life.

Afternoon: Drive to Ayodhya

After lunch, begin your journey to Ayodhya (approximately 2.5-3 hours drive), one of the seven sacred cities for Hindus and the birthplace of Lord Ram. Check into your hotel upon arrival.

En route: Stop at Bahu Begum ka Maqbara in Faizabad, a beautiful mausoleum built in 1816.

Evening: Saryu River Aarti

Visit the ghats of the Saryu River to witness the beautiful evening aarti (prayer ceremony). The illuminated ghats and the spiritual atmosphere provide a serene experience.

Dinner: Enjoy a simple vegetarian dinner at a local restaurant near the ghats, trying local specialties like Satvik food.

Day 4

Ayodhya to Prayagraj

Morning: Ayodhya Temple Tour

Start your day with a visit to the Ram Janmabhoomi complex, the birthplace of Lord Ram. Continue to other important temples including Hanuman Garhi, dedicated to Lord Hanuman, and Kanak Bhawan, known as the palace of Lord Ram and Sita.

Activity: Participate in a morning prayer ceremony at one of the temples.

Afternoon: Drive to Prayagraj

After lunch, begin your journey to Prayagraj (formerly Allahabad), approximately 4-5 hours drive. This ancient city is famous for the Triveni Sangam, the confluence of three sacred rivers: the Ganges, Yamuna, and the mythical Saraswati.

En route: Stop at Sultanpur Bird Sanctuary if time permits, especially during winter months when migratory birds visit.

Evening: Arrival in Prayagraj

Check into your hotel in Prayagraj. If time permits, take a stroll along the Civil Lines area, known for its colonial architecture and vibrant markets.

Accommodation: Kanha Shyam Hotel, Hotel Harsh Ananda, or Hotel Milan Palace for comfortable stays.

About Prayagraj

Prayagraj (formerly Allahabad) is one of the oldest cities in India and plays a significant role in Hindu mythology and history. It's known for the Triveni Sangam, where the Ganges, Yamuna, and mythical Saraswati rivers meet. The city hosts the Kumbh Mela, the largest religious gathering in the world, every 12 years. Prayagraj was also an important center during India's freedom struggle and is associated with the Nehru family.

Sacred Triveni Sangam
Host of Kumbh Mela
Rich historical significance
Day 5

Exploring Prayagraj

Morning: Triveni Sangam

Visit the sacred Triveni Sangam early in the morning. Take a boat ride to the exact point of confluence where the waters of different colors meet. Many pilgrims take a holy dip here, believing it washes away sins.

Activity: Participate in a small puja (prayer ritual) offered by local priests at the Sangam.

Afternoon: Allahabad Fort & Anand Bhavan

Visit the Allahabad Fort, built by Emperor Akbar in 1583, which houses the Akshaya Vat (immortal banyan tree) and the underground Patalpuri Temple. Then explore Anand Bhavan, the ancestral home of the Nehru family, now converted into a museum showcasing India's freedom struggle.

Lunch: Enjoy lunch at El Chico or Eat On restaurant, trying local specialties.

Evening: Allahabad University & Khusro Bagh

Drive past the historic Allahabad University, one of the oldest universities in India with beautiful colonial architecture. Visit Khusro Bagh, a walled garden complex containing the tomb of Prince Khusrau, son of Emperor Jahangir.

Dinner: Try the famous North Indian cuisine at Pind Balluchi or Sagar Ratna.

Triveni Sangam

Anand Bhavan

Day 5 Tips
  • Visit Triveni Sangam early in the morning to avoid crowds and enjoy a peaceful experience.
  • Photography is allowed at most sites, but there may be restrictions at certain religious places.
  • Allahabad Fort is under the control of the Indian Army, so security checks are strict.
  • Carry water and wear comfortable footwear as you'll be doing a lot of walking.
  • If visiting during Kumbh or Ardh Kumbh Mela, expect massive crowds and plan accordingly.
Triveni Sangam Significance

The Triveni Sangam is considered one of the most sacred places in Hinduism. It's believed that a drop of immortality nectar (Amrit) fell here during the mythical churning of the ocean. Taking a dip at this confluence is thought to cleanse one's sins and free them from the cycle of rebirth. The Kumbh Mela, held here every 12 years, attracts millions of pilgrims from around the world.

Day 6

Prayagraj to Varanasi

Morning: Hanuman Temple & Shankar Viman Mandapam

Visit the famous Hanuman Temple near Sangam, known for its unique reclining statue of Lord Hanuman. Then explore the Shankar Viman Mandapam, a multi-story temple with beautiful architecture offering panoramic views of the city.

Activity: Take a final stroll along the Sangam area to soak in the spiritual atmosphere.

Afternoon: Drive to Varanasi

After lunch, begin your journey to Varanasi (approximately 3-4 hours drive), one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world and a major spiritual center for Hindus.

En route: Stop at Vindhyachal if time permits, known for its temple dedicated to Goddess Vindhyavasini.

Evening: Arrival & Ganga Aarti

Check into your hotel in Varanasi. In the evening, head to the famous Dashashwamedh Ghat to witness the spectacular Ganga Aarti, a powerful and mesmerizing spiritual ritual performed by priests with fire and music.

Accommodation: Taj Ganges, BrijRama Palace, or Guleria Kothi for heritage stays; Ganga Fuji or Hotel Alka for mid-range options.

Ganga Aarti Tips

Day 7

Varanasi & Sarnath

Morning: Sunrise Boat Ride

Wake up early for a sunrise boat ride on the Ganges River. Witness the city coming to life as pilgrims perform their morning rituals and prayers along the ghats. See the famous burning ghats where Hindu cremation ceremonies take place.

Activity: After the boat ride, take a walking tour through the narrow lanes of the old city, visiting temples like Kashi Vishwanath (one of the 12 Jyotirlingas) and Annapurna Temple.

Afternoon: Sarnath Excursion

Visit Sarnath, located about 10 km from Varanasi, where Buddha delivered his first sermon after attaining enlightenment. Explore the Dhamek Stupa, Ashoka Pillar, and the Archaeological Museum housing Buddhist relics.

Lunch: Enjoy lunch at a local restaurant in Sarnath, trying simple vegetarian fare.

Evening: Cultural Experience

Return to Varanasi and attend a classical music or dance performance showcasing the rich cultural heritage of Varanasi. The city is known as a center for Indian classical music and dance traditions.

Dinner: Try local specialties like Banarasi Paan, Malaiyo (a seasonal milk dessert), and Tamatar Chaat at a local eatery.

About Varanasi

Varanasi, also known as Kashi or Banaras, is one of the oldest living cities in the world with a history dating back more than 3,000 years. It is considered the spiritual capital of India and attracts pilgrims from all over who come to bathe in the sacred Ganges River to wash away their sins. The city is also an important center for arts, music, and education, home to the prestigious Banaras Hindu University.

Sacred Ganges River and 88 ghats
Over 23,000 temples
Rich tradition of classical music
Day 8

Varanasi to Jaunpur

Morning: Banaras Hindu University

Visit the Banaras Hindu University campus, one of the largest residential universities in Asia. Explore the Bharat Kala Bhavan Museum housing a rich collection of Indian art, and the New Vishwanath Temple within the campus.

Activity: Take a final stroll along the ghats to soak in the spiritual atmosphere of Varanasi.

Afternoon: Drive to Jaunpur

After lunch, begin your journey to Jaunpur (approximately 1.5-2 hours drive), a historical city known for its impressive Islamic architecture from the Sharqi dynasty period.

En route: Enjoy the rural landscapes of eastern Uttar Pradesh.

Evening: Jaunpur Exploration

Check into your hotel in Jaunpur. In the evening, visit the magnificent Atala Masjid, built in 1408, known for its impressive architecture and massive central dome. Also see the Shahi Bridge, built during Emperor Akbar's reign, spanning the Gomti River.

Accommodation: Hotel Yadav or other local accommodations.

Banaras Hindu University

Atala Masjid, Jaunpur

About Jaunpur

Jaunpur was founded in the 14th century by Firoz Shah Tughlaq and later became the capital of the Sharqi dynasty. Known as the "Shiraz of India" due to its cultural and architectural significance, Jaunpur was an important center of Islamic culture and education. The city is famous for its distinctive style of mosque architecture, characterized by massive gateways (pishtaqs) and central domes.

Unique Islamic architecture
Historic Shahi Bridge
Rich literary heritage
Day 9

Jaunpur to Mathura

Morning: Jaunpur Monuments

Continue exploring Jaunpur's historical monuments. Visit the Jama Masjid, built in 1470, with its impressive gateway and absence of minarets, characteristic of the Sharqi style. Also see the Lal Darwaza Masjid and the ruins of Jaunpur Fort.

Activity: Walk through the local markets to experience daily life in this historical city.

Afternoon: Journey to Mathura

After lunch, begin your journey to Mathura (approximately 7-8 hours by train or road). Mathura is the birthplace of Lord Krishna and one of the seven sacred cities for Hindus.

Note: This is a long journey. Consider taking a train for more comfort. If traveling by road, make stops along the way to rest.

Evening: Arrival in Mathura

Check into your hotel in Mathura. If you arrive early enough, take a short stroll around the Krishna Janmabhoomi area to get a feel for this sacred city.

Accommodation: Hotel Brijwasi Royal, Radha Ashok, or Hotel Sheetal Regency for comfortable stays.

About Mathura

Mathura is one of the oldest cities in India and is considered the birthplace of Lord Krishna. Located on the banks of the Yamuna River, it's one of the seven sacred cities in Hinduism. The city has numerous temples and ghats, and its history dates back to the ancient Vedic period. Along with nearby Vrindavan, it forms the Braj region, associated with Krishna's childhood and youth.

Day 10

Mathura & Vrindavan

Morning: Krishna Janmabhoomi

Visit the Krishna Janmabhoomi Temple complex, believed to be the birthplace of Lord Krishna. Explore the temple and the prison cell where Krishna is said to have been born. Continue to Dwarkadhish Temple, dedicated to Lord Krishna as the king of Dwarka.

Activity: Take a boat ride on the Yamuna River, visiting the various ghats along the riverbank.

Afternoon: Vrindavan Temples

Drive to Vrindavan (approximately 15 km from Mathura), associated with Krishna's childhood. Visit the famous Banke Bihari Temple, ISKCON Temple, and Prem Mandir, known for its beautiful architecture and marble work.

Lunch: Enjoy a traditional satvik meal at Govinda's Restaurant near ISKCON Temple.

Evening: Cultural Experience

Return to Mathura and attend an evening aarti at Vishram Ghat, the main ghat of Mathura. If possible, watch a traditional Raslila performance, depicting stories from Krishna's life through dance and music.

Dinner: Try local specialties like Pedas (a sweet Mathura is famous for) and chaat at the local markets.

Day 10 Tips
  • Dress modestly when visiting temples. Women should cover their heads in some temples.
  • Photography may be restricted in certain temples, especially inside the sanctum.
  • Be prepared for crowds, especially during festivals and weekends.
  • Remove shoes before entering temples. Consider carrying a pair of socks.
  • Beware of monkeys around temple areas, especially in Vrindavan.
  • Try the famous Mathura Peda (sweet) from the local sweet shops.
Festivals in Mathura-Vrindavan

If your visit coincides with any of these festivals, you're in for a special experience:

  • Janmashtami (August/September): Krishna's birthday celebration
  • Holi (March): The festival of colors is celebrated with great enthusiasm
  • Radha Ashtami (August/September): Celebrating Radha's birthday
  • Braj Holi (before main Holi): Special celebrations in the Braj region
Day 11

Mathura to Agra

Morning: Govardhan Hill

Take a morning excursion to Govardhan Hill (approximately 25 km from Mathura), a sacred hill associated with Lord Krishna. Visit the Govardhan Temple and take a parikrama (circumambulation) of a portion of the hill if time permits.

Activity: Visit Kusum Sarovar, a historic stepped tank with beautiful sandstone architecture.

Afternoon: Drive to Agra

After lunch, begin your journey to Agra (approximately 1.5-2 hours drive), home to the iconic Taj Mahal. En route, you can stop at Fatehpur Sikri, the abandoned Mughal capital built by Emperor Akbar.

Fatehpur Sikri: Explore this UNESCO World Heritage Site, including the Buland Darwaza, Jama Masjid, and Salim Chishti's Tomb.

Evening: Arrival in Agra

Check into your hotel in Agra. If time permits, visit Mehtab Bagh, a garden complex located across the Yamuna River from the Taj Mahal, offering a beautiful sunset view of the monument.

Accommodation: Oberoi Amarvilas, Taj Hotel & Convention Centre, or ITC Mughal for luxury stays; Crystal Sarovar Premiere or Howard Plaza for mid-range options.

Govardhan Hill

Fatehpur Sikri

About Fatehpur Sikri

Fatehpur Sikri was built by Emperor Akbar between 1571 and 1585 and served as the capital of the Mughal Empire for about 10 years before being abandoned due to water scarcity. This UNESCO World Heritage Site is a remarkable blend of Persian, Islamic, and Hindu architectural elements. The complex includes palaces, courts, a mosque, private quarters, and other buildings, all made of distinctive red sandstone.

Buland Darwaza - one of the largest gateways in the world
Jama Masjid - one of India's largest mosques
Tomb of Salim Chishti - exquisite marble work
Day 12

Agra & Departure

Morning: Taj Mahal at Sunrise

Wake up early to visit the magnificent Taj Mahal at sunrise, when the marble changes color as the sun rises. This UNESCO World Heritage Site, built by Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal, is one of the world's most iconic monuments.

Activity: Take your time exploring the main mausoleum, gardens, and the museum. Hire a guide to learn about the fascinating history and architecture.

Afternoon: Agra Fort & Itimad-ud-Daulah

Visit the impressive Agra Fort, another UNESCO site, which served as the main residence of the Mughal emperors until 1638. Explore its palaces, audience halls, and mosques. Continue to Itimad-ud-Daulah (Baby Taj), a beautiful marble tomb that inspired the Taj Mahal.

Lunch: Enjoy lunch at a local restaurant like Pinch of Spice or Peshawri, trying Mughlai specialties.

Evening: Shopping & Departure

Spend some time shopping for Agra's famous marble inlay work, leather goods, and handicrafts. Visit Sadar Bazaar or Kinari Bazaar for souvenirs.

Departure: Transfer to Agra Cantt Railway Station or drive to Delhi (3-4 hours) for your onward journey. If flying out from Delhi, ensure you leave Agra by early evening.

Essential Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit

  • October to March: The best time with pleasant weather and minimal rainfall
  • April to June: Summer season with very high temperatures, not recommended
  • July to September: Monsoon season, humid with occasional heavy rainfall
  • Plan your visit around festivals like Diwali (Oct-Nov) or Holi (March) for a cultural experience

Transportation

  • Air: International airports at Lucknow and Varanasi; domestic airports at Agra and Prayagraj
  • Train: Excellent rail network connecting all major cities
  • Local Transport: Auto-rickshaws, cycle-rickshaws, and app-based taxis
  • Road: Good highways connect major cities, but traffic can be chaotic
  • Consider hiring a driver for the entire journey for convenience

Packing Essentials

  • Clothing: Light cotton clothes; modest attire for religious sites
  • Footwear: Comfortable walking shoes as you'll be exploring many sites
  • Accessories: Sunglasses, hat, sunscreen, insect repellent
  • Medication: Personal medications, basic first aid kit, anti-diarrheal medicine
  • Electronics: Camera, power bank, universal adapter
  • Miscellaneous: Hand sanitizer, wet wipes, toilet paper
