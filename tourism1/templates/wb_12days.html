West Bengal: A Cultural Paradise

West Bengal offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and historical significance. From the bustling streets of Kolkata to the serene tea gardens of Darjeeling, this 12-day itinerary covers the best of what this eastern Indian state has to offer.

Day 1

Arrival in Kolkata & City Exploration

Morning: Arrival & Check-in

Arrive at Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose International Airport. Transfer to your hotel in central Kolkata. Take some time to rest and refresh after your journey.

Accommodation: Taj Bengal or The Oberoi Grand for luxury stays; The Elgin Fairlawn or Kenilworth Hotel for mid-range options.

Afternoon: Lunch & South Kolkata

Enjoy your first Bengali meal at a traditional restaurant like 'Oh! Calcutta' or '6 Ballygunge Place'. Visit the Victoria Memorial, a magnificent marble building dedicated to Queen Victoria, surrounded by lush gardens.

Lunch recommendation: Try Bengali classics like Macher Jhol (fish curry), Begun Bhaja (fried eggplant), and Mishti Doi (sweet yogurt).

Evening: Cultural Immersion

Visit the iconic Howrah Bridge at sunset for spectacular views. Take a leisurely stroll along the Prinsep Ghat on the banks of the Hooghly River. Enjoy dinner at Park Street, Kolkata's food and entertainment hub.

Evening activity: If available, catch a cultural performance at Rabindra Sadan or Academy of Fine Arts.

Victoria Memorial

Howrah Bridge

Day 1 Tips
  • Carry a water bottle and stay hydrated, especially during summer months.
  • Use app-based cabs like Uber or Ola for convenient transportation around the city.
  • Victoria Memorial is closed on Mondays, plan accordingly.
  • Park Street comes alive in the evening with numerous dining options.
Day 2

North Kolkata Heritage Tour

Morning: College Street & Coffee House

Start your day with a visit to College Street, the largest book market in India. Enjoy a cup of coffee at the historic Indian Coffee House, once a meeting place for intellectuals and freedom fighters.

Activity: Browse through the numerous bookstores and pick up Bengali literature translations.

Afternoon: Marble Palace & Kumartuli

Visit the Marble Palace, a 19th-century mansion with a collection of Western art. Continue to Kumartuli, the potter's quarter, where artisans create clay idols for various festivals, especially Durga Puja.

Note: For Marble Palace, obtain a free permit from West Bengal Tourism office a day in advance.

Evening: Dakshineswar & River Cruise

Visit the Dakshineswar Kali Temple, a Hindu temple dedicated to Goddess Kali. Take an evening cruise on the Hooghly River for a different perspective of the city.

Dinner: Try Bengali street food like Kathi Rolls, Phuchka (pani puri), and Jhal Muri at New Market area.

College Street

Kumartuli Artisans

Day 2 Route

Hotel → College Street (15 min) → Indian Coffee House (walking distance) → Marble Palace (20 min) → Kumartuli (15 min) → Lunch break → Dakshineswar Temple (30 min) → River Cruise (boarding point near Millennium Park) → Hotel

Day 3

Kolkata to Sundarbans

Early Morning: Departure to Sundarbans

Early morning departure from Kolkata to Godkhali jetty (approximately 3 hours by road). Board a boat to enter the Sundarbans National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and the largest mangrove forest in the world.

Note: Pre-book your Sundarbans tour package which typically includes transportation, boat ride, meals, and guide.

Afternoon: Mangrove Safari

Enjoy a boat safari through the narrow creeks and rivers of the Sundarbans. Look out for wildlife including the Royal Bengal Tiger, crocodiles, spotted deer, wild boars, and numerous bird species.

Lunch: Most tour packages include a simple lunch served on the boat.

Evening: Accommodation in Sundarbans

Stay overnight at a lodge or eco-resort in the Sundarbans. Enjoy a cultural program showcasing local Bonobibi Pala (folk performance) after dinner.

Accommodation options: Sundarban Tiger Camp, Sunderban Jungle Camp, or Tour Operators' Cottages.

Sundarbans Mangrove Forest

Important Information
  • Carry insect repellent, sunscreen, hat, and light cotton clothes.
  • Photography is allowed but flash photography is prohibited.
  • Mobile network is limited in the Sundarbans.
  • Follow all safety instructions given by your guide.
  • The best time to visit is from November to February.
Wildlife Spotting

The Sundarbans is home to the Royal Bengal Tiger, but sightings are rare. You're more likely to see spotted deer, wild boars, monkeys, crocodiles, and various bird species. Look out for tiger paw prints in the mud!

Day 4

Sundarbans Exploration

Early Morning: Sunrise Safari

Early morning boat safari to catch the sunrise over the mangroves. This is one of the best times for wildlife spotting as animals are more active during cooler hours.

Activity: Visit a watchtower for panoramic views of the forest and potential wildlife sightings.

Afternoon: Village Visit

Return to the lodge for breakfast. Later, visit a local village to understand the life of people living in the Sundarbans region. Learn about their unique challenges and adaptations to the mangrove ecosystem.

Cultural experience: Interact with local fishermen and honey collectors to learn about their traditional practices.

Evening: Sunset Cruise

Enjoy a sunset cruise through different channels of the Sundarbans. As the sun sets, the mangrove forest takes on a magical quality with silhouettes against the orange sky.

Dinner: Enjoy a traditional Bengali dinner at your lodge, featuring fresh seafood caught by local fishermen.

About Sundarbans

The Sundarbans is the largest mangrove forest in the world, spanning across India and Bangladesh. It's named after the Sundari trees that are found in abundance here. The area is a complex network of tidal waterways, mudflats, and small islands of salt-tolerant mangrove forests.

UNESCO World Heritage Site
Home to the Royal Bengal Tiger
Largest mangrove ecosystem in the world
Day 5

Sundarbans to Digha

Morning: Return to Mainland

After breakfast, check out from your lodge and return to Godkhali jetty by boat. From there, board your pre-arranged vehicle to Digha (approximately 5-6 hours drive), West Bengal's most popular seaside resort town.

Note: The journey is long, so make sure to carry snacks and water. You'll pass through rural Bengal landscapes which offer a glimpse into village life.

Afternoon: Arrival in Digha

Arrive in Digha by late afternoon. Check into your beachside hotel and take some time to rest and refresh. Digha is known for its shallow beach that extends for miles with gentle waves, making it perfect for relaxation.

Accommodation: Hotel Seahawk, Digha Amarabati Beach Resort, or Sana Beach Resort for comfortable stays.

Evening: Beach Sunset

Head to the beach for a leisurely stroll along the shoreline. Enjoy the spectacular sunset over the Bay of Bengal. The beach comes alive in the evening with various food stalls and local vendors.

Dinner: Try fresh seafood at one of the beachside restaurants. Pomfret, prawns, and crabs are local specialties.

Sunset at Digha Beach

About Digha

Digha is the most popular sea resort in West Bengal, located about 187 km from Kolkata. Known for its gentle slope into the Bay of Bengal, Digha offers a unique beach experience with shallow water extending far into the sea. The town is divided into Old Digha and New Digha (Digha Mohana), each with its own charm.

7 km long beach with shallow waters
Famous for fresh seafood
Beautiful sunrise and sunset views
Day 6

Digha to Shantiniketan

Morning: Digha Exploration

Early morning walk on the beach to catch the sunrise. Visit the Marine Aquarium and Research Centre to see various marine species. Explore New Digha and Amarabati Park before checking out from your hotel.

Activity: If time permits, visit the nearby Udaipur Beach, which is less crowded and offers pristine views.

Afternoon: Journey to Shantiniketan

After lunch, begin your journey to Shantiniketan (approximately 5-6 hours drive). Shantiniketan, meaning "abode of peace," is where Nobel laureate Rabindranath Tagore established Visva-Bharati University.

Route: The journey takes you through the rural heartland of West Bengal, offering glimpses of village life and agricultural landscapes.

Evening: Arrival in Shantiniketan

Arrive in Shantiniketan by evening. Check into your hotel or guest house. Take a leisurely walk around the area to get a feel for this cultural hub.

Accommodation: Camellia Hotel & Resort, Mitali Homestay, or Chhuti Resort for a comfortable stay.

Marine Aquarium, Digha

Shantiniketan Campus

About Shantiniketan

Shantiniketan is a small town near Bolpur in the Birbhum district of West Bengal. It gained international fame as the place where Rabindranath Tagore established Visva-Bharati University in 1921. The town embodies Tagore's vision of an open-air school, where education would be in harmony with nature.

Home to Visva-Bharati University
Center for Bengali art and culture
Famous for Kantha embroidery and handicrafts
Day 7

Shantiniketan to Murshidabad

Morning: Explore Shantiniketan

Visit the Uttarayan Complex, which houses Rabindranath Tagore's residences - Bari (Bichitra) and Udayan. Explore the Visva-Bharati University campus and the Rabindra Bhavan Museum, which displays Tagore's personal belongings and manuscripts.

Cultural experience: If your visit coincides with Saturday, don't miss the Shantiniketan Haat (market) where local artisans sell handicrafts, Kantha embroidery, Batik prints, and terracotta items.

Afternoon: Journey to Murshidabad

After lunch, depart for Murshidabad (approximately 3-4 hours drive), the former capital of Bengal during the Mughal era. The town is known for its historical significance and architectural heritage.

En route: Pass through rural Bengal with its picturesque landscapes of paddy fields, small villages, and mango orchards.

Evening: Arrival in Murshidabad

Arrive in Murshidabad by evening. Check into your hotel and rest. If time permits, take a short walk along the banks of the Bhagirathi River to get a glimpse of the Hazarduari Palace illuminated in the evening.

Accommodation: The Bari Kothi Heritage Hotel, Hotel Manjusha, or Murshidabad Tourism Lodge for a comfortable stay.

Uttarayan Complex, Shantiniketan

Hazarduari Palace, Murshidabad

About Murshidabad

Murshidabad was the capital of Bengal, Bihar, and Orissa during the Mughal rule and later became the seat of the Nawabs of Bengal. The city reached its peak during the early 18th century and was a center of trade, culture, and politics. Today, it stands as a testament to Bengal's rich historical past with its palaces, mosques, and gardens.

Former capital of Bengal
Rich in Mughal and colonial architecture
Famous for silk weaving and ivory carving
Day 8

Murshidabad to Bishnupur

Morning: Murshidabad Sightseeing

Visit the Hazarduari Palace, a massive palace with 1000 doors (only 100 are real) built in 1837. Explore the Nizamat Imambara, one of the largest Imambaras in India, and the Katra Mosque, built in 1723.

Also visit: The Kathgola Palace and Gardens, known for its beautiful architecture and landscaped gardens.

Afternoon: Journey to Bishnupur

After lunch, begin your journey to Bishnupur (approximately 5-6 hours drive), famous for its terracotta temples built during the Malla dynasty. The town is also known for its distinctive style of architecture, handicrafts, and classical music.

En route: The journey takes you through changing landscapes of West Bengal, from the plains of Murshidabad to the laterite soil region of Bankura district.

Evening: Arrival in Bishnupur

Arrive in Bishnupur by evening. Check into your hotel and rest. If time permits, visit the local market to see Bishnupur's famous handicrafts like terracotta horses, Baluchari sarees, and conch shell carvings.

Accommodation: Hotel Akash, WBTDC Tourist Lodge, or local guesthouses for a comfortable stay.

Hazarduari Palace Interior

Terracotta Temple, Bishnupur

About Bishnupur

Bishnupur was the capital of the Malla dynasty for almost a thousand years. The town is renowned for its terracotta temples built in the distinctive Bengal style of architecture, characterized by curved roofs. Besides its architectural heritage, Bishnupur is also famous for its classical music (Bishnupur Gharana), Baluchari sarees, and terracotta crafts.

Famous for terracotta temples
Home to Bishnupur Gharana of classical music
Known for Baluchari silk sarees
Day 9

Bishnupur to Darjeeling

Morning: Bishnupur Temple Tour

Explore the famous terracotta temples of Bishnupur. Visit the Rasmancha (oldest brick temple), Jor Bangla Temple, Shyam Rai Temple, and Madanmohan Temple, all known for their intricate terracotta sculptures depicting scenes from the Ramayana and Mahabharata.

Cultural experience: Visit a local workshop to see artisans creating terracotta crafts and Baluchari sarees.

Afternoon: Journey to Kolkata

After lunch, drive to Kolkata (approximately 4-5 hours). From Kolkata, take an evening flight to Bagdogra Airport (nearest airport to Darjeeling, approximately 1.5 hours flight).

Alternative: If you prefer, you can take an overnight train from Kolkata to New Jalpaiguri (NJP) station, though this would require adjusting the itinerary.

Evening: Arrival in Darjeeling

From Bagdogra Airport, take a pre-arranged taxi to Darjeeling (approximately 3 hours drive). The winding road takes you through beautiful tea gardens and offers stunning views as you gain altitude.

Accommodation: Windamere Hotel, Mayfair Darjeeling, or The Elgin for heritage stays; Cedar Inn or Dekeling Resort for mid-range options.

Day 10

Darjeeling Exploration

Early Morning: Tiger Hill Sunrise

Very early morning departure (around 4 AM) to Tiger Hill to witness the sunrise over the Kanchenjunga range, the third highest mountain in the world. On a clear day, you might even catch a glimpse of Mount Everest.

Note: This is weather-dependent. Carry warm clothes as it can be very cold in the early morning.

Morning: Toy Train Ride

Return to the hotel for breakfast. Take a joy ride on the famous Darjeeling Himalayan Railway (DHR), also known as the "Toy Train" - a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The ride from Darjeeling to Ghum is particularly scenic.

Visit: Ghum Monastery, the oldest Tibetan Buddhist monastery in the area, and the DHR Museum.

Afternoon: Tea Estate Tour

Visit one of Darjeeling's famous tea estates like Happy Valley Tea Estate. Learn about the tea-making process from plucking to processing, and enjoy a tea tasting session.

Lunch: Enjoy lunch at Glenary's, a colonial-era bakery and restaurant serving continental and Indian cuisine.

Evening: Mall Road & Local Shopping

Explore the Mall Road (Chowrasta), the heart of Darjeeling town. Shop for Darjeeling tea, Tibetan handicrafts, and woolen items. Enjoy dinner at a local restaurant trying Tibetan and Nepali dishes.

Try: Momos (dumplings), Thukpa (noodle soup), and Sel Roti (sweet rice bread).

Day 11

Darjeeling to Dooars

Morning: Himalayan Mountaineering Institute

Visit the Himalayan Mountaineering Institute and Padmaja Naidu Himalayan Zoological Park. The zoo is home to endangered Himalayan species including the Red Panda and Snow Leopard.

Also visit: Japanese Peace Pagoda (Nipponzan Myohoji Buddhist Temple) for panoramic views of the Himalayas.

Afternoon: Drive to Dooars

After lunch, drive to the Dooars region (approximately 3-4 hours), the gateway to the northeast India and Bhutan. The region is known for its lush forests, wildlife sanctuaries, and tea gardens.

Destination: Head to Lataguri or Murti, which serve as good bases to explore Gorumara National Park.

Evening: Arrival in Dooars

Check into your jungle resort or lodge. Relax and enjoy the natural surroundings. If time permits, visit a nearby watchtower for wildlife spotting.

Accommodation: Gorumara Elephant Camp, Murti Tourism Property, or Riverwood Forest Retreats for a comfortable jungle stay.

Dooars Forest Landscape

About Dooars

The Dooars or Duars are the alluvial floodplains in northeastern India that lie south of the outer foothills of the Himalayas and north of the Brahmaputra River basin. This region is known for its rich biodiversity, dense forests, national parks, and wildlife sanctuaries. The name 'Dooars' comes from 'doors' as the region is the gateway to Bhutan and the northeastern states of India.

Home to diverse wildlife including elephants, rhinos, and tigers
Famous for tea plantations
Crisscrossed by numerous rivers and streams
Day 12

Dooars to Kolkata & Departure

Early Morning: Wildlife Safari

Early morning jeep safari in Gorumara National Park. The park is home to the Indian one-horned rhinoceros, elephants, gaur (Indian bison), and various deer species. Bird watchers will enjoy spotting numerous avian species.

Activity: Visit a watchtower for better wildlife viewing. The Jatraprasad Watch Tower and Rhino Observation Point are popular spots.

Afternoon: Return Journey

After breakfast and check-out, drive to Bagdogra Airport (approximately 3 hours). Take an afternoon flight back to Kolkata.

Alternative: If your international flight departs the next day, stay overnight in Kolkata.

Evening: Last-Minute Shopping & Departure

If time permits, do some last-minute shopping in Kolkata. New Market and Dakshinapan Shopping Centre are good options for souvenirs and handicrafts.

Departure: Transfer to Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose International Airport for your onward journey.

Essential Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit

  • Kolkata: October to March (winter months)
  • Sundarbans: November to February
  • Darjeeling & Dooars: March to May (spring) and September to November (autumn)
  • Bishnupur & Murshidabad: October to March
  • Digha: October to February for pleasant weather
  • Avoid the monsoon season (June to September) as heavy rainfall can disrupt travel plans

Transportation

  • Air: Kolkata is well-connected to major cities. Bagdogra Airport serves Darjeeling and Dooars
  • Train: Extensive rail network with New Jalpaiguri (NJP) as the gateway to the hills
  • Local Transport: App-based cabs in Kolkata; shared jeeps and taxis in hill stations
  • Road Trips: Consider hiring a driver for the entire journey for convenience
  • Toy Train: Don't miss the heritage Darjeeling Himalayan Railway experience

Packing Essentials

  • Clothing: Light cotton for plains; warm layers for Darjeeling and Dooars
  • Footwear: Comfortable walking shoes for city exploration and hiking
  • Accessories: Sunglasses, hat, sunscreen, insect repellent
  • Medication: Personal medications, basic first aid kit
  • Electronics: Camera, power bank, universal adapter
  • Miscellaneous: Binoculars for wildlife viewing, umbrella during monsoon

Food & Cuisine

West Bengal offers a diverse culinary experience:

Bengali Cuisine
  • Fish curries (Macher Jhol)
  • Shorshe Ilish (Hilsa fish in mustard sauce)
  • Kosha Mangsho (Bengali mutton curry)
  • Cholar Dal (Bengal gram lentil)
  • Mishti Doi (sweet yogurt)
  • Rosogolla and Sandesh (sweets)
Regional Specialties
  • Darjeeling: Momos, Thukpa, Sel Roti
  • Sundarbans: Fresh seafood
  • Murshidabad: Mughlai cuisine
  • Dooars: Tribal dishes
  • Street food: Kathi Rolls, Phuchka, Jhal Muri
  • Beverages: Darjeeling tea, fresh coconut water

Practical Information

  • Language: Bengali is the primary language. English is widely understood in tourist areas
  • Currency: Indian Rupee (₹). ATMs are available in cities and major towns
  • Electricity: 230V, 50Hz. Type C, D, and M plugs
  • Internet: Good connectivity in cities; limited in remote areas like Sundarbans
  • Safety: West Bengal is generally safe for tourists. Take standard precautions
  • Permits: No special permits required for Indian nationals. Foreign tourists should carry valid ID
  • Tipping: 10% is customary at restaurants if service charge is not included
  • Festivals: Durga Puja (October), Poush Mela (December), and Holi (March) are major celebrations
