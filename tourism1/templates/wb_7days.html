Day 1

Arrival in Kolkata & City Exploration

Morning: Arrival & Check-in

Arrive at Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose International Airport. Transfer to your hotel in central Kolkata. Take some time to rest and refresh after your journey.

Accommodation: Taj Bengal or The Oberoi Grand for luxury stays; The Elgin Fairlawn or Kenilworth Hotel for mid-range options.

Afternoon: Lunch & South Kolkata

Enjoy your first Bengali meal at a traditional restaurant like 'Oh! Calcutta' or '6 Ballygunge Place'. Visit the Victoria Memorial, a magnificent marble building dedicated to Queen Victoria, surrounded by lush gardens.

Lunch recommendation: Try Bengali classics like Macher Jhol (fish curry), Begun Bhaja (fried eggplant), and Mishti Doi (sweet yogurt).

Evening: Cultural Immersion

Visit the iconic Howrah Bridge at sunset for spectacular views. Take a leisurely stroll along the Prinsep Ghat on the banks of the Hooghly River. Enjoy dinner at Park Street, Kolkata's food and entertainment hub.

Evening activity: If available, catch a cultural performance at Rabindra Sadan or Academy of Fine Arts.

Day 1 Tips
  • Carry a water bottle and stay hydrated, especially during summer months.
  • Use app-based cabs like Uber or Ola for convenient transportation around the city.
  • Victoria Memorial is closed on Mondays, plan accordingly.
  • Park Street comes alive in the evening with numerous dining options.
Day 2

North Kolkata Heritage Tour

Morning: College Street & Coffee House

Start your day with a visit to College Street, the largest book market in India. Enjoy a cup of coffee at the historic Indian Coffee House, once a meeting place for intellectuals and freedom fighters.

Activity: Browse through the numerous bookstores and pick up Bengali literature translations.

Afternoon: Marble Palace & Kumartuli

Visit the Marble Palace, a 19th-century mansion with a collection of Western art. Continue to Kumartuli, the potter's quarter, where artisans create clay idols for various festivals, especially Durga Puja.

Note: For Marble Palace, obtain a free permit from West Bengal Tourism office a day in advance.

Evening: Dakshineswar & River Cruise

Visit the Dakshineswar Kali Temple, a Hindu temple dedicated to Goddess Kali. Take an evening cruise on the Hooghly River for a different perspective of the city.

Dinner: Try Bengali street food like Kathi Rolls, Phuchka (pani puri), and Jhal Muri at New Market area.

Day 3

Sundarbans National Park

Early Morning: Departure to Sundarbans

Early morning departure from Kolkata to Godkhali jetty (approximately 3 hours by road). Board a boat to enter the Sundarbans National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and the largest mangrove forest in the world.

Note: Pre-book your Sundarbans tour package which typically includes transportation, boat ride, meals, and guide.

Afternoon: Mangrove Safari

Enjoy a boat safari through the narrow creeks and rivers of the Sundarbans. Look out for wildlife including the Royal Bengal Tiger, crocodiles, spotted deer, wild boars, and numerous bird species.

Lunch: Most tour packages include a simple lunch served on the boat.

Evening: Accommodation in Sundarbans

Stay overnight at a lodge or eco-resort in the Sundarbans. Enjoy a cultural program showcasing local Bonobibi Pala (folk performance) after dinner.

Accommodation options: Sundarban Tiger Camp, Sunderban Jungle Camp, or Tour Operators' Cottages.

Sundarbans Mangrove Forest

Important Information
  • Carry insect repellent, sunscreen, hat, and light cotton clothes.
  • Photography is allowed but flash photography is prohibited.
  • Mobile network is limited in the Sundarbans.
  • Follow all safety instructions given by your guide.
  • The best time to visit is from November to February.
Wildlife Spotting

The Sundarbans is home to the Royal Bengal Tiger, but sightings are rare. You're more likely to see spotted deer, wild boars, monkeys, crocodiles, and various bird species. Look out for tiger paw prints in the mud!

Day 4

Sundarbans to Darjeeling

Morning: Sunrise Safari

Early morning boat safari to catch the sunrise over the mangroves. Visit a watchtower for panoramic views of the forest. Return to the lodge for breakfast.

Afternoon: Return to Kolkata

Return journey to Kolkata by boat and then by road. Reach Kolkata by afternoon. Quick lunch at a local restaurant.

Travel option: Take an evening flight from Kolkata to Bagdogra Airport (nearest airport to Darjeeling, approximately 1.5 hours flight).

Evening: Arrival in Darjeeling

From Bagdogra Airport, take a pre-arranged taxi to Darjeeling (approximately 3 hours drive). Check into your hotel in Darjeeling. Enjoy the cool mountain air and rest for the night.

Accommodation: Windamere Hotel, Mayfair Darjeeling, or The Elgin for heritage stays; Cedar Inn or Dekeling Resort for mid-range options.

Day 5

Darjeeling Exploration

Early Morning: Tiger Hill Sunrise

Very early morning departure (around 4 AM) to Tiger Hill to witness the sunrise over the Kanchenjunga range, the third highest mountain in the world. On a clear day, you might even catch a glimpse of Mount Everest.

Note: This is weather-dependent. Carry warm clothes as it can be very cold in the early morning.

Morning: Toy Train Ride

Return to the hotel for breakfast. Take a joy ride on the famous Darjeeling Himalayan Railway (DHR), also known as the "Toy Train" - a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The ride from Darjeeling to Ghum is particularly scenic.

Visit: Ghum Monastery, the oldest Tibetan Buddhist monastery in the area, and the DHR Museum.

Afternoon: Tea Estate Tour

Visit one of Darjeeling's famous tea estates like Happy Valley Tea Estate. Learn about the tea-making process from plucking to processing, and enjoy a tea tasting session.

Lunch: Enjoy lunch at Glenary's, a colonial-era bakery and restaurant serving continental and Indian cuisine.

Evening: Mall Road & Local Shopping

Explore the Mall Road (Chowrasta), the heart of Darjeeling town. Shop for Darjeeling tea, Tibetan handicrafts, and woolen items. Enjoy dinner at a local restaurant trying Tibetan and Nepali dishes.

Try: Momos (dumplings), Thukpa (noodle soup), and Sel Roti (sweet rice bread).

Day 6

Darjeeling to Kalimpong

Morning: Himalayan Mountaineering Institute

Visit the Himalayan Mountaineering Institute and Padmaja Naidu Himalayan Zoological Park. The zoo is home to endangered Himalayan species including the Red Panda and Snow Leopard.

Also visit: Japanese Peace Pagoda (Nipponzan Myohoji Buddhist Temple) for panoramic views of the Himalayas.

Afternoon: Drive to Kalimpong

After lunch, drive to Kalimpong (approximately 2 hours), a hill station known for its panoramic valley views, Buddhist monasteries, and colonial architecture. Check into your hotel in Kalimpong.

Accommodation: The Elgin Silver Oaks, Himalayan Hotel, or Holumba Haven for a comfortable stay.

Evening: Kalimpong Exploration

Visit Thongsa Gompa, the oldest monastery in Kalimpong. Explore the local market area and shop for traditional Tibetan handicrafts, thangka paintings, and Darjeeling tea.

Dinner: Try local Nepali cuisine at a restaurant like Gompu's or The Art CafÃ©.

About Kalimpong

Kalimpong is a hill station located in the northern part of West Bengal. It was once part of the Silk Route and a major trading post for wool trade with Tibet. The town is known for its colonial architecture, Buddhist monasteries, and flower nurseries that export exotic flowers.

Elevation: 1,250 meters (4,101 ft)
Pleasant climate throughout the year
Famous for flower nurseries and orchids
Day 7

Kalimpong to Kolkata & Departure

Morning: Kalimpong Sightseeing

Visit Dr. Graham's Homes, a school established in 1900 with beautiful colonial architecture. Explore the Durpin Monastery and Durpin Dara Hill for panoramic views of the surrounding mountains.

Also visit: Pine View Nursery, one of the many flower nurseries Kalimpong is famous for.

Afternoon: Return Journey

After lunch, drive to Bagdogra Airport (approximately 3 hours). Take an evening flight back to Kolkata.

Alternative: If your international flight departs the next day, stay overnight in Kolkata.

Evening: Last-Minute Shopping & Departure

If time permits, do some last-minute shopping in Kolkata. New Market and Dakshinapan Shopping Centre are good options for souvenirs and handicrafts.

Departure: Transfer to Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose International Airport for your onward journey.

Essential Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit

  • Kolkata: October to March (winter months)
  • Sundarbans: November to February
  • Darjeeling & Kalimpong: March to May (spring) and September to November (autumn)
  • Avoid the monsoon season (June to September) as heavy rainfall can disrupt travel plans

Transportation

  • Air: Kolkata is well-connected to major cities. Bagdogra Airport serves Darjeeling and Kalimpong
  • Train: Extensive rail network with New Jalpaiguri (NJP) as the gateway to the hills
  • Local Transport: App-based cabs in Kolkata; shared jeeps and taxis in hill stations
  • Consider hiring a driver for the hill portion of your journey

Packing Essentials

  • Clothing: Light cotton for Kolkata; warm layers for Darjeeling and Kalimpong
  • Footwear: Comfortable walking shoes for city exploration and hiking
  • Accessories: Sunglasses, hat, sunscreen, insect repellent
  • Medication: Personal medications, basic first aid kit
  • Electronics: Camera, power bank, universal adapter

Food & Cuisine

West Bengal offers a diverse culinary experience:

Bengali Cuisine
  • Fish curries (Macher Jhol)
  • Shorshe Ilish (Hilsa fish in mustard sauce)
  • Kosha Mangsho (Bengali mutton curry)
  • Cholar Dal (Bengal gram lentil)
  • Mishti Doi (sweet yogurt)
  • Rosogolla and Sandesh (sweets)
Hill Cuisine
  • Momos (dumplings)
  • Thukpa (noodle soup)
  • Gundruk (fermented leafy greens)
  • Sel Roti (sweet rice bread)
  • Darjeeling tea
  • Churpi (hard cheese)

Practical Information

  • Language: Bengali is the primary language. English is widely understood in tourist areas
  • Currency: Indian Rupee (â‚¹). ATMs are available in cities and major towns
  • Electricity: 230V, 50Hz. Type C, D, and M plugs
  • Internet: Good connectivity in cities; limited in remote areas
  • Safety: West Bengal is generally safe for tourists. Take standard precautions
  • Permits: No special permits required for Indian nationals. Foreign tourists should carry valid ID
  • Tipping: 10% is customary at restaurants if service charge is not included
